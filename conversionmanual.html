Step 01
The assembled unit
A compact polycarbonate enclosure housing two Li-ion cells, a wireless charging coil, and a USB-C core — all held together by six screws.
Step 02
Unfasten the chassis
Place the device upside down. Unscrew the six retaining screws around the bottom plate using a non-conductive tool.
Step 03
Expose the internals
Lift the flat top cover vertically to reveal the wireless coil assembly and battery carriage. Extract both cylindrical Li-ion cells from the carriage — do not crush or puncture.
Step 04
Form the stand
Insert the bottom edge of the top cover into the angled groove inside the main base block. It clicks into a stable resting angle.
Step 05
Your charging station
Connect a USB-C cable directly to the core. Your degraded power bank has become a permanent desktop dock — zero new materials required.

Full Conversion Guide

Safety instructions, step-by-step details, and e-waste guidance.

1. Critical Safety Warnings

⚠️
Hazard Identification: Lithium-ion batteries carry significant chemical and electrical risks if mishandled. Read all hazards before attempting physical conversion.
  • Thermal Runaway: Overheating or physical damage may cause the battery to explode or leak flammable contents.
  • Toxic Gas Emission: Ruptured cells emit highly toxic combustion by-products.
  • Warning Signs: If the battery emits an unusual smell, feels hot, or bubbles, stop immediately.
  • Short Circuit Vulnerability: Never connect the positive and negative terminals with a metallic object.

2. Preparation & PPE

Before opening the device enclosure, ensure you have established a safe working environment.

  • Discharge the Device: Ensure the battery is discharged to between 10% and 50% capacity.
  • Remove Conductive Items: Remove rings, watches, and metallic jewelry to prevent arcing.
  • Insulate Your Tools: Use only non-conductive tools. Do not place metal tools near the battery cells.
  • Workstation: Perform the conversion in a dry, well-ventilated area. Have a fireproof safety bag ready.

3. Safe Storage and E-Waste Routing

The extracted lithium-ion cells remain dangerous even when depleted. Please follow reverse logistics protocols:

  • Terminal Insulation: Immediately insulate both terminals of the extracted cells with adhesive tape to prevent shorting.
  • Safe Storage: Place the taped batteries into a fireproof safety bag and store in a cool, dry place.
  • Responsible Disposal: Never dispose of lithium-ion batteries in standard trash. Use our locator tool to find a certified e-waste drop-off facility.
