Phase 01 — The power bank

Premium charging,
from day one.

MagSaved snaps to your phone just like any MagSafe accessory — but underneath the surface, it's engineered differently. Built with a modular Core and Shell architecture: the magnets and induction coil live in a long-lasting Core module, while the replaceable battery sits in a separate Shell.

Unlike standard power banks sealed with industrial glue, MagSaved uses snap-fit mechanisms and standard screws — so there's never a need for heat guns or pry tools when its battery eventually degrades.
Better for your safety. Better for the environment.

Phase 03 — Extending what matters

The magnet outlives
the battery.

The valuable components in any MagSafe device are the copper induction coils and the rare earth neodymium magnet. In a standard power bank, these are glued shut; when the battery dies, they are thrown out with it, heading straight to e-waste even though they're still functional.

MagSaved is designed so the Core module, containing the entire magnet array and charging coil, survives both phases of the product's life. The battery is consumable by design. The magnets are not.

Phase 04 — End of life

The shell composts.
The core gets recycled.

Most plastic housings claim to be "biodegradable" — but PLA, the most common bioplastic, only breaks down in industrial composting facilities above 58°C, and behaves like conventional plastic in soil, seawater, and landfill.
We chose PHA instead.

PHA is a polyester naturally produced by bacteria during fermentation. Unlike PLA, it degrades in soil, home compost, freshwater, and seawater within months, leaving no microplastics behind. Critically, PHA is stable during normal use: it only biodegrades when exposed to sufficient bacterial activity, so your housing won't fall apart on a shelf.

Once the desktop dock phase ends, the PHA shell goes to compost. The neodymium Core module can be easily taken apart to ensure nothing needs to be wasted.

Material science

Why PHA, and not just any bioplastic.

🧫
Naturally produced
PHA is synthesized inside bacterial cells during fermentation. No petrochemicals required: it is a genuinely biological material, not a chemically modified fossil-fuel derivative.
🌊
Degrades in all environments
Peer-reviewed studies tracking PLA in seawater found near-zero degradation after 428 days. PHA breaks down in marine water, soil, freshwater and home compost within months without leaving microplastics.
🏠
Stable on your shelf
PHA only biodegrades when exposed to high bacterial loads. In a dry, indoor environment, like your desk or bag, it behaves as a normal, durable plastic. Your power bank won't degrade before you're done with it.
⚖️
Meets EU standards
The EU Green Claims Directive and Regulation 2025/40 on packaging waste flag PLA as a greenwashing risk. PHA meets the criteria for authentic biodegradability claims across multiple real-world disposal routes.
♻️
The complete end-of-life path
After Phase 2, MagSaved separates cleanly into three streams: the PHA shell composts at home or in soil; the extracted Li-ion cells go to a certified battery drop-off (terminals insulated, placed in a fireproof bag); and the neodymium Core module, accessible without any destructive disassembly, feeds directly into rare-earth recycling. No glue. No mixed-material waste. No guesswork.
